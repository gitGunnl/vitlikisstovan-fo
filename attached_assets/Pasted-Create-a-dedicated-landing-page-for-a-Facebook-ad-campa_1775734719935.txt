Create a dedicated landing page for a Facebook ad campaign for an AI workshop service in the Faroe Islands.

The page has one job only: get organizations to contact me about the workshop. The conversion actions are:
1. submit the contact form
2. contact me by email
3. call me

Do not treat this like a normal homepage. It should feel like a focused campaign landing page with no distractions, very clear message match to a Facebook ad, and a calm, practical tone. The audience is managers, directors, HR people, department heads, and other buyers inside Faroese organizations who are considering a practical AI workshop for office staff.

This is not a technical AI course. It is a hands-on 3-hour workshop for office staff and knowledge workers. The core promises of the page are:
- practical starting point for all office staff
- a clearer idea for each person about what tasks they should use AI for and where not to
- clearer judgment about what is safe to share

The overall feeling should be:
clear, grounded, practical, trustworthy, local, professional, and simple
Not flashy, not futuristic, not hype-driven, not overdesigned, and not like a SaaS product launch page.

The page should be mobile-first in feel because much of the Facebook traffic will likely arrive on mobile. It should also look strong on desktop. The page should scan easily, feel fast, and make the next step obvious right away.

DESIGN AND UX INTENT
The page should feel:
- credible
- calm
- practical
- easy to scan
- human
- not salesy
- not crowded

The visual hierarchy should make the hero, form, and CTA extremely clear. The page should feel made for normal working people in organizations, not for tech enthusiasts.

Avoid:
- flashy AI visuals
- futuristic clichés
- noisy gradients everywhere
- excessive animation
- lots of navigation
- multiple competing calls to action
- generic corporate stock-page feel

Good visual direction:
- clean layout
- strong spacing
- readable typography
- soft but confident styling
- subtle cues of trust
- realistic, grounded feel

If an image is used, it should support the feeling of a practical workshop for office staff, not abstract AI imagery.

IMPORTANT
Keep all text content easy to edit later.
Use placeholders where social proof or contact details need to be inserted later.
Structure the page so it is easy to swap in real testimonials, logos, phone number, and email.
The page should be ready to use as a dedicated Facebook landing page.
