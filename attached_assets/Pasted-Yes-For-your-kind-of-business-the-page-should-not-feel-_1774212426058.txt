For your kind of business, the page should not feel like “AI consultant for everything.” It should feel like: “We come in, make AI practical for your staff, and leave them actually able to use it on Monday.” The strongest landing pages keep the message clear, make the next step obvious, and build trust with proof instead of vague claims. I would build your page around one main truth: your workshop is not about “the future of AI.” It is about helping normal staff use these tools safely and usefully in daily work. That is much more concrete, and it is easier to buy.

One important copy choice

Do not overuse the word “AI.” The page should be about outcomes, not the topic itself. People are not buying AI. They are buying staff who understand how to use new tools well, save time, and avoid mistakes. Pages that stay focused on the visitor’s goal tend to work better than pages that mainly talk about the provider.  ￼

My blunt advice

Your homepage should probably not lead with “agency,” “consulting,” or “advisory.” It should lead with the workshop, because that is the proven offer. Then the rest of the page should quietly show that you can help them with the next step too.

That keeps the message sharp and credible.
